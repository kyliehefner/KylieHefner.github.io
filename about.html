About Me

A data scientist with a passion for modeling, mathematics, and meaningful insights

I hold a Master’s degree in Computational and Data Sciences from Chapman University and a Bachelor’s in Mathematics for Teaching from UCLA. My work sits at the intersection of applied math, machine learning, and high-performance computing.

Over the last few years, I’ve completed 15+ projects in areas like natural language processing, numerical simulation, image restoration, and statistical modeling. I enjoy solving challenging problems and translating data into real-world insights — especially when it involves creative modeling and technical depth.

I’m especially interested in roles that are research-heavy and technical, where I can work independently while contributing to a larger mission. My favorite moments come from digging deep into the math behind models and optimizing systems that support fast, accurate computation.

Beyond the Data

Outside of work, I enjoy knitting, reading, and exploring new ways to apply math in unexpected places. I believe in learning for its own sake and in building things that are both elegant and useful.
